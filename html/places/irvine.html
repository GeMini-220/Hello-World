The morning is pale.
A quiet road curves between low hills.
Sprinklers tick in the grass.
A cyclist passes without a sound.
Palm shadows stretch across the pavement.
A distant freeway murmurs—
then a door closes somewhere.

The sun hangs over empty parking lots.
White buildings wait behind trimmed trees.
No one seems to hurry.

It sees. It let them pass.
The hills are just lying there.
They never moved.

But the wind is moving.
Where is the wind going?

No one knows.
